**Q: What is an aura?**

**A:** The aura, also known as the human energy field, is a manifestation of the universal energy field. It is a luminous body that surrounds and permeates the physical body, radiating unique, emanating light. The aura is a part of the human being that is connected to the universal energy field.

According to observations, the aura is divided into several layers, sometimes referred to as energy “bodies.” These layers interpenetrate one another and surround each other in sequence. The outer layers are composed of more subtle matter and exhibit higher “vibrational” frequencies.

The aura consists of seven primary layers. Each layer has a distinct appearance and function, and each is associated with a corresponding chakra:

* The first layer and the first chakra relate to physical functions and sensations such as pain or pleasure, connecting with the body’s automatic mechanisms and autonomic nervous system.
* The second layer and the second chakra are associated with human emotions, serving as the medium for emotional life and feelings.
* The third layer and the third chakra relate to the mind and linear thinking.
* The fourth layer and the fourth chakra (heart chakra) are the medium of love, encompassing not only romantic love but also love for humanity as a whole.
* The fifth layer and the fifth chakra are associated with higher will, closer to divine will, involving the power of speech, listening, and responsibility for actions.
* The sixth layer and the sixth chakra relate to divine love, a form of love that transcends human love and nurtures all life.
* The seventh layer and the seventh chakra are associated with the higher mind, involving the recognition and integration of our spiritual and physical structures.

Within the aura, there is a main vertical energy current that pulses up and down along the field of the spinal nerves, extending above the head and below the tailbone. The chakras are conical vortices located along this energy current.

Therefore, non-physical experiences such as sensations, emotions, thoughts, and memories all have their place within the energy system. Understanding the aura helps bridge traditional medicine and psychological care, offering insights into the nature of health and illness.

You can begin to perceive the aura through simple exercises. For example, in a dimly lit room, hold your fingertips facing each other and relax your gaze. Many people can see a mist-like energy around the fingertips, which may appear colorless or bluish, and can stretch or shift like taffy as the fingers move.
